The Crab Nebula

The Crab is considered to be one of the most stable astrophysical sources, and it has been used for the calibration of multiple missions as it is bright in the high-energy regime. Despite the Crab Nebula's expected stability in flux, the pulsar's activity doesn't significantly alter its spectrum, suggesting a remarkable resilience to fluctuations in the central pulsar's behavior. In this study, we use the Swift Burst Alert Telescope's (BAT) capability of storing event data solely during Gamma Ray Burst (GRB) occurrences for analyzing the Crab system. This study presents a unique opportunity for explaining the hard X-ray emission from the Crab Nebula, providing valuable insights into its complex processes.
